
# Useless ID

## Overview
Useless ID is an Israeli punk rock band formed in Haifa in 1994. The band is widely regarded as one of the most internationally successful punk bands to emerge from Israel, and one of the most visible acts from the broader Middle Eastern punk scene.

## Formation and Early Years (1994–1999)
The band formed in 1994 and began as part of the local Israeli punk underground, performing in small venues and self-releasing early recordings. In its early period, the band operated independently and released material through its own label, **Falafel Records**, named after a food commonly associated with the band’s members.

During this period, Useless ID built a reputation through intensive live performances and early touring activity, including international shows that helped establish connections outside Israel.

## International Breakthrough (1999–2003)
A turning point for the band came in the late 1990s when they were invited to record a split release with The Ataris. This led to increased visibility in the American punk scene and a subsequent signing with **Kung Fu Records**.

Their 2001 album *Bad Story, Happy Ending* marked an important shift, including stronger international production and broader distribution. This period also established the band’s connection to the North American punk circuit and DIY touring culture.

## Continued Output and Evolution (2003–2012)
Useless ID continued releasing albums and touring internationally throughout the 2000s. Albums such as *No Vacation from the World* (2003) and *The Lost Broken Bones* (2008) expanded their sound toward melodic punk and pop-punk influences.

The band became associated with labels such as:
- Kung Fu Records  
- Suburban Home Records  
- Fat Wreck Chords  

During this time, lineup changes occurred, particularly in the drummer position, but the core songwriting and identity remained stable.

## Later Period and Continued Activity (2012–present)
With the release of *Symptoms* (2012) and later *State Is Burning* (2016), Useless ID continued to refine a fast, melodic punk style while maintaining strong ties to the global punk and hardcore touring networks.

The band remains active and continues to be associated with international punk circuits, particularly in Europe and North America.

## Style and Influence
Useless ID is generally associated with:
- Melodic punk rock
- Skate punk
- Pop-punk influences with hardcore elements

Their work is often described as bridging Israeli underground punk with the broader international punk scene.
